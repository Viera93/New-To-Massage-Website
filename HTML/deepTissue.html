Deep Tissue Massage

Deep tissue massage is a technique that focuses on the deeper layers of muscles and aims to release tension in the body. Technique of this massage is usually slower but the pressure is deeper and concentrated on the areas, where the patient feels tension and pain. Practitioner is usually using his/hers wrist and elbow without any oil. The purpose of this massage is to loosen tightened fibres of a muscle, eliminate blockages while releasing the build-up of tension and pain.

Deep tissue massage offers physical and psychological benefits. Unlike other massages, that focus on relaxation, Deep tissue massage helps to treat muslce pain and improve stiffness AND it can still help you to unwind mentally too.

Who can benefit?

  • Deep tissue massage reduces chronic, muscle cramping and increase mobility.
  • It helps ease stress and tension, which can have a beneficial effect on blood pressure
  • It can help break up and eventually erase scar tissue in the body.
  • It gives effective treatment for injured muscles and also helps in reducing pain caused by injuries.

Deep Tissue massage is recomonded by many doctors as a treatment option to cure a variety of physical ailments. Because Deep tissue massage increases the flow of blood through the body, it helps reduce the inflamation that causes pain.

What are the contraindications for Deep tissue massage?

  • Injuries (sprains/strains) within acute inflammatory stage. After this stage, very light massage can speed recovery time and healing process.
  • Varicose veins, thrombosis - if you take a blood thinners as a medical precaution against clotting, deep tissue massage could cause dislodging a thrombus.
  • Open wounds within acute stage. Again, after this stage, massage can limit scar tissue.
  • Autoimmune diseases - when the immune system produces antibodies against the body`s own tissues. Pressure on inflamed tissues can be aggravating

One of the side effects after receiving a Deep tissue massage is, that your body might be a bit sore and you might experience mild bruising. In general, infections and undiagnosed pain should be checked by a medical doctor before booking yourself an appointment. ALSO, your therapist should be informed of your conditions.
